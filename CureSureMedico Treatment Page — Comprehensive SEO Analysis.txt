🔴 Priority 1 — Technical SEO (Fix Before Launch)
1. Server-Side Rendering (SSR) or Static Site Generation (SSG)
Your page appears to be a fully client-side rendered React/Next.js app. Content only appears after JavaScript executes. Google can crawl JS, but it's unreliable and delays indexing by weeks.
Action: Since you're using Next.js (evident from the meta tag), use getServerSideProps or getStaticProps for all treatment pages. Every piece of treatment content — title, description, doctors, costs — must be present in the initial HTML response.
2. Metadata Per Treatment Page
Each treatment page needs unique, keyword-rich tags:
html
<title>Kidney Stone Treatment in India | Cost, Hospitals & Doctors | CureSureMedico</title>
<meta name="description" content="Get affordable kidney stone treatment in India starting from $X. 
Compare top hospitals, experienced urologists, and all-inclusive packages. Free consultation available." />
Karetrip's title format works well: [Procedure] in [Country] | [Brand]. Follow a similar pattern but differentiate with your multi-country, Africa-focused angle.
3. Canonical Tags
Ensure every treatment page has a self-referencing canonical to avoid duplicate content across similar condition pages.
4. Page Speed
Medical tourism patients research extensively — slow pages lose them. Compress all WebP images (Karetrip uses CloudFront CDN), lazy-load below-the-fold content, and aim for a Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) under 2.5s.

🟢 Priority 4 — E-E-A-T Signals (Google's Trust Framework)
Medical pages fall under Google's "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL) category — meaning Google holds them to a much higher trust standard. You need to demonstrate Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness.
What Karetrip does: Named testimonials, named doctors, named hospitals, Google rating badge.
What you should add that Karetrip lacks:
* An "About Our Medical Team" section on each page — who vets the hospitals you recommend?
* Author bylines on any medical content — ideally a named medical advisor
* Accreditation logos prominently displayed (NABH, JCI, ISO)
* A "Last medically reviewed" date on each page
* Links to external authoritative sources (WHO, peer-reviewed journals) within the content
* A clear disclaimer ("CureSureMedico is a medical facilitation company, not a healthcare provider")

🔵 Priority 5 — Schema Markup (Structured Data)
Implement these JSON-LD schemas on every treatment page. Karetrip likely has basic schema; this is where you can outperform them:
json
{
  "@type": "MedicalProcedure",
  "name": "Laparoscopic Nephrectomy",
  "description": "...",
  "bodyLocation": "Kidney",
  "followup": "...",
  "howPerformed": "..."
}
Also implement: FAQPage, Physician (for each doctor), Hospital (for each hospital), Review/AggregateRating, and BreadcrumbList.
